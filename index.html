The distance is real

Millions of Filipino parents work a full day away in time zone alone, before the plane ticket ever enters the picture. Kalinga doesn't close that gap — it just makes sure your presence still arrives on schedule.

01 · Shared Timeline

One feed, two ends of a day

The caregiver at home posts what actually happened — the scraped knee, the spelling test, the nap that went long. The parent abroad reacts and replies whenever their shift allows. No pressure to be online at the same time.

02 · Async Presence

Record now, arrive on their time

A bedtime message recorded during a lunch break in Riyadh is delivered at bedtime in Manila — not the moment it's sent. Presence, scheduled around the child's day rather than the parent's.

03 · Find Your Words

Help starting, never help pretending

When you don't know how to say it, Kalinga offers a starting line — never a finished message. It nudges you toward your own words. It doesn't write the moment for you.

04 · Honest Presence

Gaps stay visible, on purpose

A quiet week shows up as a quiet week — to you, not just to the caregiver. Kalinga won't let a scheduled message stand in for a parent who's checked out. It's built to be a habit worth keeping, not an alibi.
